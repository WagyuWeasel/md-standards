# Tight feedback loops accelerate learning more than practice volume

The rate of improvement in any skill depends more on the speed and
quality of feedback than on raw hours of practice. A programmer who
runs tests after every change learns faster than one who writes code
for an hour and then debugs. The same pattern appears in music
(recording yourself), sports (video review), and writing (immediate
peer review). Anders Ericsson's deliberate practice research confirms
this: practice without feedback is just repetition, not learning.
The implication for tool design is that the best developer tools
minimize the gap between action and feedback.

## Source

Ericsson, A., *Peak: Secrets from the New Science of Expertise*
(2016). Chapter 4.
